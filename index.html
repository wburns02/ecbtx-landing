Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about document digitization services

What is document digitization?

Document digitization is the process of converting paper records into searchable digital files. We use high-speed scanners and OCR (Optical Character Recognition) technology to transform your physical documents into organized, searchable PDFs that can be accessed from any device, backed up to the cloud, and searched instantly by keyword.

How much does document scanning cost?

Document scanning costs vary based on volume, document condition, and required processing. For most Texas county projects, pricing ranges from $0.05 to $0.15 per page depending on complexity. We provide free, no-obligation quotes after reviewing your specific needs. Contact us at (979) 236-1958 for a custom estimate.

What types of documents can you digitize?

We digitize all types of documents including septic/OSSF permits, government records, legal documents, medical records (HIPAA compliant), business contracts, historical archives, maps, engineering drawings, and more. We handle everything from standard letter-size to large-format documents up to 36 inches wide.

How long does document digitization take?

Turnaround time depends on project size and complexity. A typical county records project of 10,000-50,000 pages takes 2-4 weeks. Rush services are available for urgent needs. We provide detailed timelines in our project proposals and keep you updated throughout the process.

Is my data secure during digitization?

Absolutely. We use AES-256 encryption for all digital files, secure facilities with access controls, and all staff sign confidentiality agreements. Your documents never leave our secure chain of custody. We comply with Texas records management laws and can meet HIPAA, CJIS, and other regulatory requirements.

Do you serve my county in Texas?

Yes! We serve all 254 Texas counties with a focus on the Hill Country and Central Texas regions including Blanco, Hays, Travis, Williamson, Gillespie, Kendall, Comal, Kerr, Bastrop, and surrounding areas. We can pick up and deliver documents or work on-site at your facility.

What is an OSSF permit and why digitize it?

OSSF (On-Site Sewage Facility) permits are Texas regulatory documents for septic systems. Many counties have decades of paper OSSF records that are difficult to search and at risk of damage. Digitizing these records makes permit lookups instant, enables compliance tracking, and protects irreplaceable historical data.

Can you integrate with our existing software?

Yes, we can format digitized documents to integrate with most document management systems, GIS platforms, and county software. We export in standard formats (PDF, TIFF, CSV) and can customize metadata fields to match your existing database structure. Our team has 18+ years of IT experience.
